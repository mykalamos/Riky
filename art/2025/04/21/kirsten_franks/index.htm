Kirsten Franks

In Another Life, We Will Sit Blissfully At The Water As The Slither of Many Tentacles Lead The Way
TODO - x x y x z cm

This wall mounted installation strikes the viewer with its undulating form of etched steel. Brackets and screws secure the bottom with a looser attachment of nails the top. The beaten irregular shaping speaks of a decay. The tentacles defy gravity.

Representing the oceans with sunlight reflecing off the surface the humour of the piece is using our industrial legacy as the medium to represent the primal element: Water

The work has a microcosmic quality. Relatively small in size though potentially scalable to produce a vast aggregate work.

The title hints at transcendence - both physical and psychological - renewal and possibly even redemption through a rebalancing. A heralded reclamation of the oceans by those who once flourished there and humankind attentive at the shore line once again.

This work is part of a larger exhibition called 'Nebulous - Blurring Boundaries' curated by QLoud Collective and hosted at the compact Purist Gallery in London
